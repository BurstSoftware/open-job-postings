PROJECT REQUIREMENTS 

What to Create
Build a business that operates with AI (each a "Project").

The Project must fall within at least one of the following Categories:

Education & Human Potential 
Transforming how we learn, grow, and achieve our best. Workforce upskilling, alternative credentialing, personalized learning. 

Entrepreneurship & Job Creation 
Fueling the tools that help new founders and economies thrive. 

Small Business Services 
Powering everyday businesses with tools to compete and win. 

Money & Financial Access 
Breaking down barriers to banking, capital, and financial freedom. 

Professional Services Access 
Connecting everyday people with the expert guidance they need.

Functionality 
The entry must demonstrate / explain how AI is transforming workflows in their business. This must be depicted in the video and/or expressed in the text description. Examples include how an AI agent is used for customer support or how AI tools are used to create marketing assets.

Platforms 
A Project must use at least one product from Google Cloud. The Project must run on the platform for which it is intended and which is specified in the Submission Requirements.

New Projects 
Only Projects must be newly created by the Entrant after the start of the Hackathon Submission Period. If pre-existing templates, frameworks, boilerplates, or code were used, Entrants must explain how their Project utilized that pre-existing work.

Third Party Integrations If a Project integrates any third-party SDK, APIs and/or data, the Entrant must be authorized to use them in accordance with any terms and conditions or licensing requirements of the tool.

LLM Model Use Projects that include LLM functionality must use the Gemini API for at least one LLM call in the deployed application. Teams may use additional LLM providers alongside Gemini at their discretion.

Provide a URL to your code repository for judging and testing. The repository must contain all necessary source code. The repository must be either public (with relevant licensing) or private and shared with testing@devpost.comand judging@hacker.fund.

Include a text description of how your Project meets the above requirements. This should also explain the relevance to the chosen Category.
Include a demonstration video of your Project. The video portion of the Submission:
* should be less than three (3) minutes — judges are not required to watch beyond three minutes;
* should include footage that shows the Project functioning on the device for which it was built;
* must be uploaded to and made publicly visible on YouTube, Vimeo, or Youku, with a link provided on the submission form; and
* must not include third-party trademarks or copyrighted music/material unless the Entrant has permission to use such material.

Provide revenue evidence and explanations of the following:
* Total Revenue. Total revenue earned from arms-length third-party customers during the Hackathon period, in USD.
* Revenue by Month. Revenue broken out by calendar month: May, June, July, and August 2026.
* Total Costs. Total costs incurred during the Hackathon period, excluding marketing and customer acquisition spend (reported separately). Include a one-sentence description of what these costs cover (e.g., hosting, AI API usage, contractor fees).
* Marketing and Customer Acquisition Spend. Total spend on marketing and customer acquisition during the Hackathon period. Must be disclosed even if zero.

Provide user evidence that includes:
* Evidence of real users such as the number of individual users and a high-level breakdown of who your users are. Provide any testimonials or feedback provided by customers of your project. Please ensure your users are aware that their information is being shared.
* Related-Party Revenue. Any revenue earned during the Hackathon period from team members, family, related entities, or pre-existing customer relationships. Reported separately from Total Revenue so judges can assess whether the underlying business serves arms-length third-party customers.

Provide evidence of your product running. This includes agent execution logs, API usage records, and screenshots of dashboards — anything that strengthens the case that playbooks are running in production continuously.
