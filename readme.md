## Hypothesis / About the Project

### Inspiration
Indeed sucks. LinkedIn sucks.

### What It Does
**Open Job Listings** is a platform that surfaces **real, currently open job postings** that are actually hiring in the United States economy. It cuts through the noise of fake postings, ghost jobs, and broken application funnels that dominate the current job board landscape.

### How We Built It
We are using AI (primarily Google Gemini) to research and identify the optimal framework **from first principles**, drawing inspiration from physics-based systems thinking to model the job market as a complex, dynamic system rather than relying on legacy job board architectures.

### Challenges We Ran Into
Unknown at this stage. The project officially started on **July 2, 2026**.

### Accomplishments We're Proud Of

### What We Learned

### What's Next for Open Job Listings

## Market Analysis: Probabilities of the Market and Its Outcomes

### The Job Market / Workplace Market in 2026

The job boards are broken in America.

- Indeed posts fake jobs.
- LinkedIn posts fake jobs.
- Real job postings for open roles have too many applicants.
- Real job postings for open roles don’t have the necessary HR professionals to reach out to all applicants and read through applications in a timely manner.
- HR teams must sift through tens, hundreds, or thousands of applications.
- AI / ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) have poorly built algorithms.
- The application interface for candidates is highly technical, monotonous, tedious, and not user-friendly.
- Applying for jobs on Indeed or LinkedIn has become a full-time job in itself.
- HR and hiring managers have no requirement to follow up in a timely manner or in good faith. They can simply ghost candidates.
- The current job board tool stack is clunky.
- The job board tool stack makes businesses vulnerable to sensational reviews and adverse reporting.
- AI / ATS resume and applicant tracking systems function as black boxes.
- The number of actual interviews and jobs procured by Americans through good-faith engagement on Indeed and LinkedIn is **>1%** of total application volume.

## Core Research Areas

### 1. Job Categories
**Research Question:**  
What are the job categories in each region for which Indeed and LinkedIn operate within the United States?

### 2. Job Saturation
**Research Question:**  
What is the job saturation per job category, per region, in the markets where Indeed and LinkedIn operate in the United States?

### 3. Indeed Current Systems Analysis
**Current systems include:**
- Website
- Web app
- Forms, data, links, backend
- Graphical user interface
- User actions: search, click, input data, fill out forms, take tests/questionnaires

**Role in the marketplace:**  
Indeed serves as an intermediary between small and medium businesses and job seekers. It runs ads for businesses to attract employees.

**Problem it attempts to solve:**  
Connecting employers with candidates at scale.

### 4. LinkedIn Current Systems Analysis
**Current systems include:**
- Website
- Web app
- Forms, data, links, backend
- Graphical user interface
- User actions: search, click, input data, fill out forms, take tests/questionnaires

**Additional layers:**
- Social media platform for business (with limited effectiveness)
- Subscription model for enhanced communication, resume boosting, and extra functionality
- Ads for businesses to attract employees

**Role in the marketplace:**  
Professional networking + job board hybrid.

**Problem it attempts to solve:**  
Professional networking combined with recruitment.

## Project Philosophy

We believe the current job discovery infrastructure is fundamentally misaligned with both job seekers and employers. By approaching the problem from **first principles** (inspired by physics systems modeling) and leveraging modern AI agents on Google Cloud, we aim to build a more transparent, efficient, and trustworthy layer on top of the U.S. job market.
